A credit card usually has 16 numbers. These numbers are not random but have special meanings. The first digit is known as the Major Industry Identifier. It tells what type of industry issued the card. The next five numbers are the Issuer Identification Number, showing which bank issued the card. The following nine numbers are the account number unique to the cardholder. The last digit is called the check digit. It is there to check if the credit card number is valid or not. So, all together, there are 16 numbers on a credit card!
