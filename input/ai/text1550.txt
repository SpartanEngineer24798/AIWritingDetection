The Declaration and Resolves of the First Continental Congress, held in 1774, were essentially a diplomatic response crafted by the 13 British American Colonies. They were aiming to specify their objections to the British Parliament's Intolerable Acts and also establish their rights as Englishmen. Each resolution articulated their grievances and request for redress succinctly. Significantly, they also declared their loyalty to the British Crown, making an attempt to avoid outright rebellion. Yet, interestingly, they also implored the power of collective resistance. Think of it as a polite yet firm prod at the British Crown, asserting their autonomy without completely severing the tie.
