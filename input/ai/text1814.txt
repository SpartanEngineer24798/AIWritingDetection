ARB medications, or Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers, are treatments used primarily for high blood pressure and heart failure. They operate by blocking angiotensin, a natural substance that narrows blood vessels and releases hormones that raise blood pressure. By inhibiting the receptor, these blockers prohibit angiotensin from exerting its effects, helping the vessels to widen and the blood pressure to decrease. Some commonly employed ARBs include Losartan, Valsartan, and Irbesartan.
